Summary

The sources discuss the reasons for India's independence in 1947, specifically focusing on the role of World War II. Historians debate whether the British voluntarily withdrew due to India's declining economic value, as argued by Judith Brown, or if independence was a result of the Indian nationalist movement's decades of struggle, as argued by Chandra et al. However, the text ultimately supports the argument that the Second World War was the primary factor in India's independence, citing factors such as Britain's economic strain, the rise of the United States as a global power, and a shift in British public opinion away from imperialism. The sources emphasize how World War II weakened Britain's ability and will to maintain control over India, ultimately leading to its independence.
